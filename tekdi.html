Ganesh Tekdi Mandir

    Overview

    Ganesh Tekdi Temple also known as "Tekdicha Ganapati" means Ganpati of hills. Situated atop a hill on Station Road, this temple is about 250 years old and has immense religious significance for Hinduism. The construction of the temple started in 1978 and was completed in 1984. Late Ganpatrao Joshi and other devotees enthusiastically participated in the construction of Shri Ganesh Tekdi Temple. The presiding deity of the temple is Lord Ganesha and it is believed that the idol of the Lord is Swayambhu, who originated from the land itself.

    Facts about Ganesh Tekdi Mandir

    • It is a very quiet place, ideal for those who like to worship in solitude, away from the hustle and bustle of the city.
    • The idol of Lord Ganesha's head is decorated with gold and there are other silver ornaments.
    • Earlier, Tekdi Ganapati Temple used to be a simple temple with a tin shade and a small platform. Later, it was taken over by the Defense Department and restoration began in the year 1970.
    • It is the favourite place of Master Blaster Sachin Tendulkar to visit in Nagpur.
    • People have unwavering faith in the deity and come here with reverence in the hope of fulfilling their vows and wishes.
